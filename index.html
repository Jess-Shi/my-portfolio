About Me

AboutMe

It is a truth universally acknowledged (at least by fans of science fiction), that sufficiently advanced technology is virtually indistinguishable from magic, and that magic is simply technology that is insufficiently understood. I am driven to understand. And as trite and na√Øve as it may sound, I believe in the infinite potential of technology to change this world for the better, despite its equally vast potential for harm if misused. So here I am. And here you are.

I go by many names, but in a professional setting please call me Jess. I am a developer, a programmer, a thinker, a dreamer, and above all else, a firm believer in humanity's destiny among the stars. As a developer, I strive to build accessible, responsive websites of uncompromising quality, using all that I have recently learned from the Juno College of Technology's immersive Web Development bootcamp. It is my goal to contribute, in however small a way, to the creation of an Internet and a world that is safer, more equitable, and more welcoming to all.

This path is yet new to me, and every day brings fresh enlightenment. To someone from a non-tech background, coding is a bit like magic, and an increase in understanding makes it no less magical. I shall forever remember that feeling when I first printed "Hello, world!" to the console, and now I gaze back and marvel at how far I've come in how brief a time, and prepare to climb the next mountain.

I hope we may become fellow travellers upon this journey. In all that we may come to do together, I solemnly promise to never do less than my very best, and to reach, always, for the stars.
